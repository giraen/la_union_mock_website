    GOV. RAFY EMPHASIZES IMPORTANCE OF RELIABILITY AMONG GOV'T EMPLOYEES; ADMINISTERS MASS OATH-TAKING, RECOGNITION OF EMPLOYEES

    By: Charissa Marie E. Go, GPC-HRMU

    /

    May 8, 2023

    Photo by: PIO

    The whole Provincial Government of La Union (PGLU) looks forward to the monthly First Monday Flag-raising Ceremony as it marks a new beginning for the PGLU Community. Earlier today, May 8, 2023, Gov. Raphaelle Veronica "Rafy" Ortega-David led the first Monday flag raising ceremony for the month of May. Attended by PGLU employees, the ceremony resounded the value of reliability that everyone must exert as public servants.

    Photo by: PIO

    In her message, Gov. Rafy reiterated the value of reliability among the PGLU employees. She emphasized how each employee plays a vital role in providing the services that the public expects from the government. Gov. Rafy encouraged everyone to work together and deliver their best to fulfill their duties. To highlight the province's commitment to quality service, Gov. Rafy administered the mass oath-taking of 20 newly appointed PGLU employees. She reminded them to be reliable at all times and to uphold their responsibilities as public servants.

    From the Provincial Social Welfare and Development Office, newly appointed employees were Nicholas G. Perlas, Provincial Government Assistant Department Head; Zenaida D. Sellem, Social Welfare Officer IV; Roelita C. Asprin, Administrative Officer IV; Lerma L. Navarro, Social Welfare Officer I; Edwin S. Corpuz, Social Welfare Aide; Jeffrey F. Gomez, Social Welfare Aide; Christine C. Deang, Social Welfare Aide; Rhoda C. Fontanilla, Social Welfare Aide; Lorna D. Nieveras, Social Welfare Aide; and John O. Apilado, Administrative Aide I.

    From the Environment and Natural Resources Office, Diana E. Jutba, Senior Environmental Management Specialist; Jorrainne Nor P. Rasay, Environmental Management Specialist II; Marvin O. Aquino, Environmental Management Specialist I; and Lourmie B. Cabanela, Administrative Aide IV.

    From the Provincial General Services Office, Rhoman G. Rimando, Administrative Officer V; and Gerlyn C. Laconsay, Laborer I. From the Provincial Disaster Risk Reduction Management Office, Ric A. Nisperos and Efren A. Pulido Jr., both Administrative Aide IV.

    From Bacnotan District Hospital, Adonis N. Abenojar and from the Office of the Provincial Treasurer, Kimberly Frances N. Marquez both as Laborer I.

    Further, Filma A. Salazar, a retiring employee from the Office of the Provincial Assessor, was acknowledged for her contributions during her 41 years of service in the PGLU. Gov. Rafy thanked her for her unwavering service and commitment to the people of La Union throughout the years.

    The first Monday Flag Raising Ceremony of the PGLU is an activity that reminds employees of their mutual responsibility to provide quality service to the people of La Union. It is also an opportunity to foster a sense of community and instill La Union PROBINSYAnihan among workers as they take part in the realization of the vision for La Union to be the Heart of Agri-Tourism in Northern Luzon by 2025.

    LGUS LEADING IN ENVIRONMENTAL MANAGEMENT INITIATIVES RECOGNIZED

    By: Aubrey G. Medrano, GPC-ENRO

    /

    May 8, 2023

    Photo by: PIO

    As the Provincial Government of La Union continues to pilot initiatives for a sustainable environment, Gov. Raphaelle Veronica "Rafy" Ortega-David led the conferment of the winners of the Search for the Cleanest, Safest and Greenest (CSG) Local Government Unit (LGU) of La Union for C.Y. 2021 on May 8, 2023 at the Provincial Capitol, City of San Fernando, La Union. Validated by the CSG-Provincial Search Committee (PSC), the winners for the annual Search for the Cleanest, Safest, and Greenest LGU in the Province of La Union were Naguilian, Bacnotan, and Agoo for the first to third class municipalities, while for the fourth to fifth class municipalities the winners were Santo Tomas, Burgos, Caba, and Santol.

    Photo by: PIO

    For the Municipal Bodies of Water Coastal Category, the winners were Immuki Island (Balaoan), Urbiztondo Surfing Area (San Juan) and San Carlos Coastal Area (Caba) while in the Fresh Water Category the winners were Bulalakaw Falls (Bagulin), Baroro River (Bacnotan) and Tapuakan River (Pugo).

    To recognize the efforts of the different Urban and Rural Brgys, Best Magsidalus iti Arubayan (MIA) Barangay Implementer was included as a category. The winners from the Urban Brgy. were Brgy. Quirino (Bacnotan), Brgy. Gana (Caba) and San Nicolas East (Agoo) and for the Rural Brgy, the winners were Brgy. Ubagan (Sto.Tomas), Up-Uplas (Sudipen) and Brgy. Narra (Bacnotan).

    The Search for the Cleanest, Safest, and Greenest LGU aims to mobilize all sectors of the society in transforming and helping their communities into clean, safe, and green areas; promote a venue for people’s participation in community development efforts; and to institutionalize a system of providing recognition to the cleanest local government units throughout the country.

    To give recognition to LGU’s accomplishments by attaining best practices/activities in making the environment safer and healthier to live in, cheques were given to all the CSG Winners that will support the implementation of their various environmental management projects primarily address environmental problems such as pollution (air, water and land) and climate change.

    Gov. Rafy fully supports the Search for the Cleanest, Safest, and Greenest Project as it is geared towards the attainment of the vision of the province to become the Heart of Agri-tourism in Northern Luzon by 2025 through maintaining the cleanliness of its LGUs and promotion of environmental awareness and protection.

    LA UNION: PROBINSYANG NAGBABAYANIHAN

    By: Dayanella R. Jucutan, PIO

    /

    May 4, 2023

    Photo by: Provincial Information Office

    The Provincial Government of La Union, together with the Philippine National Volunteer Service Coordinating Agency, conducted the Institutionalization of Volunteerism as a Development Strategy Training Workshop on May 4, 2023.

    Photo by: Provincial Information Office

    Gov. Raphaelle Veronica “Rafy” Ortega-David highly regarded this activity as it emphasized the importance of volunteerism in order to attain the goals of the province and to empower the camaraderie between all kaprobinsiaan. The activity served as a platform to discuss volunteerism in a deeper sense. Basic Concept of Volunteerism and its status in the province were discussed. Initiatives of La Union towards volunteerism were also tackled, presenting its vital position to build a harmonious community.

    As a Volunteer La Union Lifeguard and a Volunteer Rescue Technician, Gov. Rafy expressed her warm support towards the advocacy. “As we conduct this training-workshop, sana ay mas malinang pa yung knowledge natin regarding volunteerism and ma-imbibe pa natin ang mga ito for us to further empower volunteerism here in La Union,” Gov. Rafy stated. This served as an encouragement to everyone to join together in volunteering and giving service to the kaprobinsiaan to answer the call for #LaUnionPROBINSYAnihan.

    PAROLEES TAKE PART IN #KALIKASANNAMAN

    By: Grace Libong, GPC-ENRO

    /

    May 3, 2023

    Photo by: Provincial Information Office

    The Provincial Government of La Union (PGLU), through the Provincial Government-Environment and Natural Resources Office (PG-ENRO), has taken a bold step towards preserving nature by signing a Memorandum of Agreement with the La Union Province Parole and Probation Office (LUPPO) in fostering ecological integrity and a clean and healthy environment in line with the PGLU's #KalikasanNaman Campaign.

    Photo by: Provincial Information Office

    The signing of the MOA took place on May 3, 2023, at the La Union Provincial Capitol lobby, City of San Fernando, La Union. The agreement entails providing forest native seedlings and a 2-hectare lot area located inside the Damortis Provincial Nursery to the LUPPO for seedling propagation and enrichment planting activities of their parolees or probationers.

    This partnership between the PG-ENRO and LUPPO highlights the importance of #LaUnionPROBINSYAnihan in achieving a common goal for the betterment of the environment and community. Gov. Raphelle Veronica “Rafy” Ortega-David in her message emphasized the significance of collective efforts in preserving nature and restoring natural resources. She reiterated that restoring the environment and natural resources is one of the highest forms of environmental stewardship and a constitutional duty of each and everyone.

    The PGLU continues to show its unwavering commitment to environmental sustainability and protection as it becomes the Heart of Agri-Tourism in Northern Luzon by 2025.

    KAYALI HOSPITALITY SERVICES INC. PAYS COURTESY VISIT TO GOV. RAFY

    By: PIO

    /

    May 2, 2023

    Photo by: PIO

    Gov. Rafy welcomes a possible collaboration with Kayali Hospitality Services, Inc. after a short but very productive meeting with them, together with representatives from the La Union Provincial Tourism Office, on May 2, 2023.
